COMETS

Comets are icy celestial bodies that orbit the Sun.

When they come close to the Sun, heat causes the ice to vaporize,

releasing gas and dust that form a glowing coma and a long tail.

Nucleus

Solid icy core
at the center.

Coma

Glowing envelope
around the nucleus.

Dust Tail

Made of tiny
dust particles.

Ion Tail

Made of charged
particles.

METEORS

Meteors are space rocks that enter Earth's atmosphere at very high speeds.

Friction with the air causes them to heat up and glow, creating bright streaks of light in the sky.

We often call these beautiful flashes "shooting stars".

Ionized Trail

A trail of ionized gas left
behind as the meteor travels.

Hot Plasma

Extreme heat caused by friction
with the atmosphere creates plasma.

Meteor

The visible streak of light caused by a
space rock burning in the atmosphere.

Meteoroid

A small rocky or metallic
body traveling in space.

TYPES OF METEORS (CLICK TO EXPLORE)
Shooting Star

A small meteor that burns quickly creating a streak of light.

Fireball

A very bright, intense meteor, brighter than Venus.

Meteorite

A space rock that survives the fall and reaches Earth.

Meteor Shower

Many meteors appearing to radiate from the same point.

ASTEROIDS

Asteroids are rocky objects that orbit the Sun.

They are smaller than planets and have irregular shapes.

Most of them are found in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter.

Surface

Rocky outer layer.

Craters

Formed by repeated impacts.

Irregular Shape

Not perfectly round.

Composition

Made of rock, metal,
and carbon materials.

ASTRONOMICAL BELT

The Astronomical Belt is a dense, torus-shaped region located between the orbits of Mars and Jupiter. It acts as a massive boundary in our Solar System, occupied by millions of solid, irregularly shaped rocky bodies and minor planets that continuously orbit the Sun.

GRAVITY

Gravity is not a force pulling you—it is space-time curving around mass.
